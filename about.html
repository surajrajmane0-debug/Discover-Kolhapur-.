Kolhapur — The Cultural Crown of Maharashtra

About Kolhapur

Kolhapur, known as the “Cultural Capital of Southern Maharashtra”, is a city that gracefully blends history, devotion, and modern progress. Nestled along the banks of the Panchganga River, it is most famous for the sacred Mahalaxmi Temple, one of India’s most revered Shakti Peethas.

The city’s royal heritage dates back to the Maratha Empire and the rule of the Bhosale dynasty. Kolhapur’s culture thrives in its art, jewelry, and cuisine — from the iconic Kolhapuri Chappals and Kolhapuri Saaj to the fiery Tambda–Pandhra Rassa and Misal.

Today, Kolhapur stands as a vibrant center of heritage, faith, and enterprise. Its people, temples, and traditions continue to reflect the city’s enduring spirit — a living testament to Maharashtra’s royal and cultural pride.
